Since \(p^*\) is a group homomorphism, to show it is injective it suffices to show that \(\ker p^*=\{e\}=\{[e_{e_0}]\}\text{.}\) (Recall that the identity element of a fundamental group \(\pi_1(X,x_0)\) is \(e=[e_{x_0}]\text{.}\)) To this end, suppose \([f]\in \pi_1(E,e_0)\) satisfies \(p_*([f])=[\pi\circ f]=[e_{b_0}]\in \pi_1(B,b_0)\text{.}\) It follows from the uniqueness property of liftings that \(f=\widetilde{p\circ f}\) and \(e_{e_0}=\widetilde{e_{b_0}}\text{.}\) Since \(p\circ f\simeq_p e_{b_{0}\text{,}\) it follows from (3) of TheoremÂ 1.4.3 that \(f\simeq_p e_{e_0}\text{,}\) and thus \([f]=[e_{e_0}]\text{.}\) This proves that if \(p_*([f])=e \text{,}\) then \([f]=e\text{:}\) i.e., \(\ker p_*=\{e\}\text{.}\)

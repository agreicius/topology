Corollary 1.12.7. Graphs of continuous functions.

Let \(f\colon X\rightarrow Y\) and let \(\Gamma_f=\{(x,f(x))\in X\times Y\colon x\in X\}\text{,}\) the graph of \(f\text{.}\) If \(f\) is continuous, then \(\Gamma_f\) is connected.

Proof.

Define \(F\colon X\rightarrow \rightarrow X\times Y\) as \(F(x)=(\id_X(x),f(x))=(x,f(x))\text{.}\) Since the two component functions \(\id_X\colon X\rightarrow X\) and \(f\colon X\rightarrow Y\) are continuous, so is \(F\text{.}\) From TheoremÂ 1.12.6, we conclude that \(F(X)=\Gamma_f\) is continuous.

Given an abelian group \(A\) the map
\begin{align*} \Hom(\bigoplus A_\alpha,A) \amp\longleftrightarrow \prod\Hom( A_\alpha,A) \\ \phi \amp \mapsto (\phi\circ i_\alpha)_{\alpha\in I} \end{align*}
is a bijection. In other words, given any abelian group \(A\) and family of group homomorphisms \(\phi_\alpha\colon A_\alpha\rightarrow A\text{,}\) there is a unique homomorphism \(\phi\colon \bigoplus A_\alpha\rightarrow A\) satisfying \(j_\alpha=\phi\circ i_\alpha\) for all \(\alpha\in I\text{.}\)
In fact, in this case we must have
\begin{equation*} \phi((g_\alpha)_{\alpha\in I})=\sum_{\alpha\in I}\phi_\alpha(g_\alpha)\text{.} \end{equation*}

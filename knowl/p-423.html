The universal mapping property characterizes \(\prod G_\alpha\) up to isomorphism: i.e., if \(G\) is a group equipped with group homomorphisms \(\pi_\alpha'\colon G\rightarrow G_\alpha\) for which the recipe
\begin{align*} \Hom(H, G) \amp\longleftrightarrow \prod\Hom(H,G) \\ \phi \amp \mapsto (\pi_\alpha'\circ \phi)_{\alpha\in I} \end{align*}
is a bijection, then \(G\cong \prod G_\alpha\text{.}\)

Lemma 1.10.6. Comparing \(S^2\) and \(\R^2\) components.

Fix \(P\in S^2\) and let \(h\colon S^2-\{P\}\rightarrow \R^2\) be a homeomorphism. Let \(C\) be a compact subspace of \(S^2\) not containing \(P\text{,}\) and let \(U\) be a component of \(S^2-C\text{.}\)
  1. If \(P\notin U\text{,}\) then \(h(U)\) is a bounded component of \(\R^2-h(C)\text{.}\)
  2. If \(P\in U\text{,}\) then \(h(U-\{P\})\) is an unbounded component of \(\R^2-h(C)\text{.}\)
  3. If \(S^2-C\) has \(n\) components, then \(\R^n-h(C)\) has \(n\) components.

Proof.

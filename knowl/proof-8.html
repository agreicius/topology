Proof

  1. It is clear that the rule \(f\mapsto h\circ f\) defines a map from \(P(X;x_0,x_0)\) to \(P(Y; y_0, y_0)\text{.}\) We must show that it is well defined on equivalence classes. Assume \(f,f'\in P(X; x_0, x_0)\) are path homotopic, and let \(F\) be a path homotopy from \(f\) to \(f'\text{.}\) It is easy to see that \(h\circ F\) is a path homotopy from \(h\circ f\) to \(h\circ f'\text{.}\) Thus \([f]=[f']\) implies \([h\circ f]=[h\circ f']\text{,}\) showing \(h_*\) is a well-defined map from \(\pi_1(X,x_0)\) to \(\pi_1(Y,y_0)\text{.}\)
    Now we show \(h_*\) is a homomorphism of groups. We will use the (easy to show fact) general fact that given \(f, g\in P(X; x_0, x_1)\) and continuous \(h\colon X\rightarrow Y\text{,}\) we have \(h\circ (f*g)=(h\circ f)*(h\cirg g)\text{.}\) Now, given \([f],[g]\in \pi_1(X, x_0)\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{align*} h_*([f]*[g]) \amp = [h\circ (f*g)]\\ \amp = [(h\circ f) * (h\circ g)\\ \amp =[h\circ f]*[h\circ g]\\ \amp =h_*([f])*h_*([g])\text{.} \end{align*}
  2. For any \(f\in \pi_1(X,x_0)\) we have
    \begin{align*} (k\circ h)_* ([f]) \amp= [(k\circ h)\circ f] \\ \amp = [k\circ (h\circ f)]\\ \amp =k_*([h\circ f])\\ \amp =k_*(h_*([f]))\\ \amp =k_*\circ h_*([f])\text{.} \end{align*}
    This proves \((k\circ h)_*=k_*\circ h_*\text{.}\)
  3. For any \([f]\in \pi_1(X,x_0)\) we have \(\id_*([f])=[\id\circ f]=[f]\text{.}\) This proves \(\id_*\) is the identity homomorphism on \(\pi_1(X, x_0)\text{.}\)

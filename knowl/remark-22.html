Remark 1.21.11. The smaller the subgroup, the bigger the covering.

Fix an element \(b_0\) of the simply connected, path connected, and locally path connected space \(B\text{.}\) Let \(G=\pi_1(B,b_0)\text{.}\) The correspondence between subgroups of \(G\) and covering spaces of \(B\) “pays attention” to the subgroup relation in the following sense:
  • Assume \(H\) and \(H'\) are subgroups of \(G\text{,}\) and let \(p_H\colon E_H\rightarrow B\) and \(p_{H'}\colon E_{H'}\rightarrow B\) be coverings corresponding to \(H\) and \(H'\) as described in [cross-reference to target(s) "th_universal_covering_space" missing or not unique]. We have \(H\leq H'\) if and only if there exists a map of coverings \(q\colon E_{H}\rightarrow E_H \text{.}\)

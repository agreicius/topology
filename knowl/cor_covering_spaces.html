Corollary 1.21.4. Covering spaces.

Let \(p\colon E\rightarrow B\) and \(p'\colon E'\rightarrow B\) be coverings. Assume \(E\text{,}\) \(E'\text{,}\) and \(B\) are path connected and locally path connected.
  1. Let \(p(e_0)=b_0\) and \(p'(e_0')=b_0'\text{.}\) There is a covering \(h\colon E\rightarrow E'\) satisfying \(p=p'\circ h\) and \(h(e_0)=e_0'\) if and only if
    \begin{equation*} p_*(\pi_1(E,e_0))\leq p_*'(\pi_1(E',e_0'))\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Furthermore, the covering \(h\) is unique in this case.
  2. Let \(p(e_0)=b_0\) and \(p'(e_0')=b_0'\text{.}\) There is a covering \(h\colon E\rightarrow E'\) satisfying \(p=p'\circ h\) if and only if the subgroup \(p_*(\pi_1(E,e_0))\) is contained in a conjugate of the subgroup \(p_*'(\pi_1(E',e_0'))\text{.}\)

Proof.

Corollary 1.17.6. One-point compactification.

Let \(X\) be locally compact Hausdorff.
  1. If \(X\) is not compact, then the inclusion map
    \begin{equation*} i\colon X\rightarrow X^* \end{equation*}
    is a one-point compactification of \(X\text{,}\) and \(X^*\) is Hausdorff.
  2. If \(X\) is not compact, and \(\iota\colon X\rightarrow Y\) is a one-point compactification where \(Y\) is Hausdorff, then there exists a unique homeomorphism \(\phi\colon X^*\rightarrow Y\) such that \(\iota\vert_X=\phi\circ i\vert_X\text{.}\)

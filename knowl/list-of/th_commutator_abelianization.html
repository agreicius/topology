Theorem 2.14.9. Commutator and abelianization.

Let \(G\) be a group.
  1. \([G,G]\) is a normal subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
  2. The quotient \(G/[G,G]\) is abelian.
  3. Let \(q\colon G\rightarrow G/[G,G]\) be the quotient map. The group \(G/[G,G]\) is the largest abelian quotient of \(G\text{,}\) as defined by the following universal property: if \(A\) is an abelian group and \(\phi\colon G\rightarrow A\) is a homomorphism, there is a unique homomorphism \(\overline{\phi}\colon G/[G,G]\rightarrow A\) satisfying \(\phi=\overline{\phi}\circ q\text{.}\)

Theorem 2.3.6. Comparing metric topologies.

Let \(d, d'\) be two metrics on the set \(X\text{,}\) and let \(\mathcal{T},\mathcal{T}'\) be the topologies they induce.
  1. We have \(\mathcal{T}\subseteq \mathcal{T}'\) if and only if every \(d\)-metric ball is a union of \(d'\)-metric balls.
  2. We have \(\mathcal{T}=\mathcal{T}'\) if and only if every \(d\)-metric ball is a union of \(d'\)-metric balls and every \(d'\)-metric ball is a union of \(d\)-metric balls.

Proof.

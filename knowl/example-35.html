Example 1.12.8.

Let \(G=\bigoplus\limits_{\alpha\in I} A_\alpha\)
  1. Consider the system of maps \(i_\alpha\colon A_\alpha\rightarrow G\text{.}\) According to the universal mapping property, there is a unique map \(\phi\colon G\rightarrow G\) satisfying \(i_\alpha=\phi\circ i_\alpha\) for all \(\alpha\text{.}\) What is \(\phi\text{?}\)
  2. Fix \(\alpha_0\in I\) and consider the system of maps \(j_\alpha\colon A_\alpha\rightarrow A_{\alpha_0}\) defined as \(j_{\alpha}=0_{A_\alpha}\) for \(\alpha\ne \alpha_0\) (the zero map), and \(j_{\alpha_0}=\id_{A_{\alpha_0}}\text{.}\) According to the universal mapping property, there is a unique map \(\phi\colon G\rightarrow A_{\alpha_0}\) satisfying \(j_\alpha=\phi\circ i_\alpha\) for all \(\alpha\text{.}\) What is \(\phi\text{?}\)

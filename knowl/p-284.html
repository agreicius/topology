The lifting correspondence \(\phi\) gives rise to an injective map
\begin{align*} \overline{\phi}\colon H\backslash\pi_1(B,b_0) \amp \hookrightarrow p^{-1}(b_0)\\ \overline{[f]} \amp \mapsto \phi([f])\text{.} \end{align*}
Here \(H\backslash \pi_1(B,b_0)\) is the set of all right cosets of \(H\text{:}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} H\backslash \pi_1(B,b_0)=\{H*[f]\colon [f]\in \pi_1(B,b_0)\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Furthermore the map \(\overline{\phi}\) is bijective if \(E\) is path connected.

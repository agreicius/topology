The condition \(x\sim x' \iff g(x)=g(x')\) guarantees that the function \(f\colon X^*\rightarrow Y\) is injective. The condition of \(g\) being a quotient map is equivalent to \(f\) being a quotient map according to Theorem 2.11.5. Thus the two conditions taken together are equivalent to \(f\) being a bijective quotient map. This in turn is equivalent to being a homeomorphism by equivalent statement 1.b of Theorem 2.9.7, since all subsets of \(X\) are saturated with respect to an invertible function \(f\text{.}\)

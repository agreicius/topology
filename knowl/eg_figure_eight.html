Example 1.9.7. Fundamental group of the figure eight.

Let \(X\) be your favorite incarnation of the figure eight space.
  1. Show that the diagram below describes a covering map of \(X\text{.}\) Your argument can be somewhat informal (since no formulas are provided).
  2. Let \(x_0\) be the intersection of the two circles in the figure eight, and let \(f,g\in P(X; x_0,x_0)\) be the simple paths traversing the left and right hoops of \(X\text{,}\) with orientation as in the diagram. Use the lifting correspondence to show that \([f]*[g]\ne [g]*[f]\text{,}\) and hence that \(\pi_1(X,x_0)\) is a nonabelian group.

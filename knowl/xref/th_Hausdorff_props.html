Theorem 1.5.7. Hausdorff properties.

Let \(X\) be a Hausdorff space.
  1. All singletons \(\{x\}\subseteq X\) are closed.
  2. All finite subsets of \(X\) are closed.

Proof.

  1. Fix any \(x\in X\text{.}\) For any \(y\ne x\text{,}\) there exist disjoint open sets \(U, V\) containing \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) respectively. In particular, we have \(V\cap \{x\}=\emptyset\text{.}\) It follows that \(y\notin \overline{\{x\}}\text{.}\) and thus that \(\overline{\{x\}}=\{x\}\text{.}\) Thus \(\{x\}\) is closed.
  2. This follows easily since a finite union of closed sets is closed.

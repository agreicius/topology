Item 3.

The universal mapping property characterizes \(\bigoplus A_\alpha\) among abelian groups up to isomorphism: i.e., if \(G\) is an abelian group equipped with group homomorphisms \(i_\alpha'\colon A_\alpha\rightarrow G\) for which the recipe
\begin{align*} \Hom(G,A) \amp\longleftrightarrow \prod\Hom(A_\alpha,A) \\ \phi \amp \mapsto (\phi\circ i_\alpha')_{\alpha\in I} \end{align*}
is a bijection for any abelian group \(A\text{,}\) then \(G\cong \bigoplus A_\alpha\text{.}\)

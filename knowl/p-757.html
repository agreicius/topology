The \(T_1\) is called a separation axiom, or Trennungsaxiom in German: hence the ‘T’. To see more explicitly why this is called a separation axiom, note that (a) \(T_1\) is equivalent to the property that any singleton is closed, and (b) this in turn is equivalent to saying for any two elements \(x\ne y\) there is an open neighborhood \(U\) of \(y\) that does not contain \(x\text{;}\) we think of this \(U\) as separating \(x\) and \(y\text{.}\)

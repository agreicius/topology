Corollary 1.15.5. Seifert-van Kampen: special cases.

Let \(X=U\cup V\) be an open covering where \(U\text{,}\) \(V\text{,}\) and \(U\cap V\) are path connected. Let \(j_1,j_2\) be as in the Seifert-van Kampen theorem.
  1. If \(U\) and \(V\) are simply connected, then \(X\) is simply connected.
  2. If \(U\cap V\) is simply connected, then \(\pi_1(X,x_0)\cong \pi_1(U,x_0)* \pi_1(V,x_0)\text{.}\)
  3. If \(V\) is simply connected, then \(\pi_1(X,x_0)\cong \pi_1(U,x_0)/N\text{,}\) where \(N\) is the least normal subgroup of \(\pi_1(U,x_0)\) containing \(i_1(\pi_1(U\cap V, x_0))\text{.}\)

Proof.

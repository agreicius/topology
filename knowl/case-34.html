Finite products.

Using induction it suffices to show that if \(X, Y\) are connected, then \(X\times Y\) is connected. First, choose any \(x_0\in X\) and note that the subspace \(\{x_0\}\times Y\text{,}\) being homeomorphic to \(Y\text{,}\) is connected. Similarly, for any \(y_0\in Y\text{,}\) the subspace \(X\times \{y_0\}\) is connected. It follows from (2) of TheoremÂ 2.12.5 that the set \(T_{x_0,y_0}=\{x_0\}\times Y\cup X\times \{y_0\}\) is connected for any \((x_0,y_0)\in X\times Y\text{.}\) Lastly, since \(T_{x,y_0}\cap T_{x',y_0}\supseteq X\times\{y_0\}\ne \emptyset\) for any \(x,x'\in X\text{,}\) the union \(\bigcup_{x\in X}T_{x,y_0}\) is connected. But this union is \(X\) itself.

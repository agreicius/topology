That (1) implies (2) follows from Theorem 2.17.2. For the converse, assume \(X\) is homeomorphic to the open set \(U\subseteq Y\text{,}\) where \(Y\) is compact and Hausdorff. We may use the homeomorphism to identify \(X\) with \(U\text{:}\) thus we think of \(X\subseteq Y\) as an open subspace of \(Y\text{.}\) Now, since \(Y\) is compact, it is locally compact. Hence by Corollary 2.17.7, given any \(x\in X\) we can find an open \(V\ni x\) such that \(\overline{V}\subseteq X\) and \(\overline{V}\) is compact. This shows that every \(x\in X\) has a compact neighborhoof \(K=\overline{V}\subseteq X\text{,}\) as desired.

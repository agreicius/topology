Theorem 1.14.10. Compactness and closed maps.

Let \(f\colon X\rightarrow Y\) be continuous. If \(X\) is compact and \(Y\) is Hausdorff, then \(f\) is a closed map.

Proof.

Let \(C\subseteq X\) be closed. Since \(X\) is compact, \(C\) is compact. By Theorem 1.14.9, \(f(C)\) is compact. By Theorem 1.14.7, since \(Y\) is Hausdorff, \(f(C)\) is closed.

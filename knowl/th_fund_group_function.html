Theorem 1.2.6. Fundamental group functor.

  1. Let \(h\colon (X,x_0)\rightarrow (Y,y_0)\) be a map of pointed spaces. The rule \(h_*([f])=[h\circ f]\) defines a group homomorphism \(h_*\colon \pi_1(X,x_0)\rightarrow \pi_1(Y,y_0)\text{.}\)
  2. Given maps of pointed spaces \(h\colon (X,x_0)\rightarrow (Y,y_0)\) and \(k\colon (Y,y_0)\rightarrow (Z,z_0)\text{,}\) we have \((k\circ h)_*=k_*\circ h_*\text{.}\)
  3. Let \(\id\colon X\rightarrow X\) be the identity function \(\id(x)=x\) for all \(x\in X\text{.}\) For any \(x_0\in X\text{,}\) we have \(\id_*=\id\text{,}\) the identity group homomorphism on \(\pi_1(X,x_0)\text{.}\)

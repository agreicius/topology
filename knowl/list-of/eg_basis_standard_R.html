Example 1.2.8. A basis for the standard topology on \(\R\).

Show that the standard topology on \(\R\) is the topology generated by the basis \(\mathcal{B}_1\) in Example 1.2.4.
Solution.
Let \(\mathcal{T}\) be the topology generated by \(\mathcal{B}_1\) and let \(\mathcal{T}'\) be the standard topology on \(\R\). Since every open interval \((a,b)\) is open in \(\mathcal{T}'\) each element of \(\mathcal{B}_1\) is an element of \(\mathcal{T}'\text{.}\) By Theorem 1.2.7, we have \(\mathcal{T}\subseteq \mathcal{T}'\text{.}\) Next, take any open set \(U\in\mathcal{T}'\text{;}\) then by definition for all \(x\in U\) there exists \(\epsilon_x> 0\) such that \((x-\epsilon_x, x+\epsilon_x)\subseteq U\text{.}\) It follows that \(U\in \mathcal{T}\) by (1.2.2). This proves \(\mathcal{T}'\subseteq \mathcal{T}\text{,}\) and thus that \(\mathcal{T}=\mathcal{T}'\text{,}\) as desired.

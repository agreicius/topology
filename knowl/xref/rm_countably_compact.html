Remark 1.16.6. Countably compact space.

A close look at our proof of TheoremÂ 1.16.5 reveals that limit point compactness follows from a slightly weaker notion of compactness: namely, countable compactness. A space is countably compact if every countable open subcover has a finite subcover. We have the following implications:
\begin{equation*} X \text{ compact} \implies X \text{ countably compact} \implies X \text{ limit point compact}\text{.} \end{equation*}
It can be shown that none of these implications is in fact an equivalence. However, if \(X\) is further assumed to be \(T_1\text{,}\) then limit point compactness implies countable compactness. We record this fact schematically as follows:
\begin{equation*} X \text{ limit point compact} \underset{(T_1)}{\implies} X \text{ countably compact}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Remark 1.1.2. Finite intersection of open sets.

Let \((X,\mathcal{T})\) be a topological space. Axiom (3) of DefinitionÂ 1.1.1 says that the intersection of any two open sets of \(X\) is again open. It follows easily by induction that the intersection of any finite list of open sets is open. In other words:
\begin{equation*} U_1,U_2,\dots, U_n\in \mathcal{T}\implies \bigcap_{i=1}^n U_i \in \mathcal{T}\text{.} \end{equation*}

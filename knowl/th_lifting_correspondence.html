Theorem 1.4.5. Lifting correspondence.

Let \(p\colon E\rightarrow B\) be a covering map. Fix \(e_0\in E\text{,}\) let \(b_0=p(e_0)\text{,}\) and let
\begin{align*} \phi\colon \pi_1(B,b_0) \amp \rightarrow p^{-1}(\{b_0\})\\ [f] \amp \mapsto \widetilde{f}(1) \end{align*}
be the resulting lifting correspondence.
  1. The group homomorphism \(p_*\colon \pi_1(E,e_0)\hookrightarrow \pi_1(B,b_0)\) is an injection.
  2. Let \(H=p_*(\pi_1(E,e_0))=\im p_*\text{.}\) We have \(\phi([f])=e_0\) if and only if \([f]\in H\text{.}\)
  3. The lifting correspondence \(\phi\) gives rise to an injective map
    \begin{align*} \overline{\phi}\colon H\backslash\pi_1(B,b_0) \amp \hookrightarrow p^{-1}(b_0)\\ \overline{[f]} \amp \mapsto \phi([f])\text{.} \end{align*}
    Here \(H\backslash \pi_1(B,b_0)\) is the set of all right cosets of \(H\text{:}\) i.e., \(H\setminus \pi_1(B,b_0)=\{H*[f]\colon [f]\in \pi_1(B,b_0)\}\text{.}\) The map \(\overline{\phi}\) is bijective if \(E\) is path connected.

Proof of (1).

Proof of (2).

Proof of (3).

Let \(\{A_\alpha\}_{\alpha\in I}\) be a family of subgroups of the abelian group \(G\text{.}\) The inclusion maps \(j_\alpha\colon A_\alpha\rightarrow G\) give rise to a unique group homomorphism \(\phi\colon \bigoplus A_\alpha\rightarrow G\text{.}\)
  1. Sum of subgroups.
    The group \(G\) is the of the \(A_\alpha\text{,}\) denoted \(G=\sum A_\alpha\text{,}\) if \(\phi\) is surjective.
  2. Direct sum of subgroups.
    The group \(G\) is the of the \(A_\alpha\text{,}\) denoted \(G=\bigoplus A_\alpha\text{,}\) if \(\phi\) is an isomorphism.

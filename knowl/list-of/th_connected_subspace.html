Theorem 1.12.6. Connectedness and subspaces.

Let \(X\) be a topological space.
  1. If \(Y\) is a connected subspace of \(X\text{,}\) and if \(X=U_1\cup U_2\) is a separation of \(X\text{,}\) then \(Y\subseteq U_1\) or \(Y\subseteq U_2\text{.}\)
  2. If \(\{Y_i\}_{i\in I}\) is a collection of connected subspaces of \(X\) and \(\bigcap_{i\in I}Y_i\ne\emptyset\text{,}\) then \(Y=\bigcup_{i\in I}Y_i\) is connected.
  3. If \(Y\subseteq X\) is connected, then any \(Y'\) satisfying \(Y\subseteq Y'\subseteq \overline{Y}\) is connected.

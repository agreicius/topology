Remark 1.21.7. Universal covering space.

Let \(p\colon (E,e_0)\rightarrow (B,b_0)\) be a universal covering.
If \(p'\colon (E',e_0')\rightarrow (B,b_0)\) is another universal covering, then we have \(\pi_1(E,e_0)=\pi_1(E',e_0')=\{e\}\) and thus \(p_*(\pi_1(E,e_0))=p_*'(\pi_1(E', e_0'))=\{e\}\text{.}\) It follows from Theorem 1.21.2 that \(E\) and \(E'\) are equivalent as covering spaces . This justifies the “the” in Definition 1.21.5.
Similarly, given any covering \(p'\colon (E',e_0')\rightarrow (B,b_0)\text{,}\) since
\begin{equation*} p_*(\pi_1(E,e_0))=\{e\}\subseteq p_*'(\pi_1(E',e_0'))\text{,} \end{equation*}
Corollary 1.21.4 implies there is a unique covering \(h\colon E\rightarrow E'\) satisfying \(p=p'\circ h\) and \(h(e_0)=e_0'\text{.}\) This justifies the “universal” in Definition 1.21.5.

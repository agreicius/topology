Theorem 1.12.6. Universal property of the direct sums.

Let \(\{G_\alpha}_{\alpha\in I\}\) be a family of abelian groups.
  1. Inclusion maps.
    For each \(\alpha\in I\) the map \(i_\alpha\colon G_\alpha\rightarrow \oplus G_\alpha\) is a group homomorphism.
  2. Universal mapping property.
    Given an abelian group \(H\) the set \(\Hom (\oplus G_\alpha,H)\) is in bijective correspondence with the set \(\prod \Hom(G_{\alpha},H)\) via the recipe
    \begin{align*} \Hom(\oplus G_\alpha,H) \amp\longleftrightarrow \prod\Hom( G_\alpha,H) \\ \phi \amp \mapsto (\phi\circ i_\alpha) \end{align*}
  3. The universal mapping property characterizes \(\prod G_\alpha\) up to isomorphism: i.e., if \(G\) is a group equipped with group homomorphisms \(\pi_\alpha'\colon G\rightarrow G_\alpha\) for which the recipe
    \begin{align*} \Hom(H, G) \amp\longleftrightarrow \prod\Hom(H,G) \\ \phi \amp \mapsto (\pi_\alpha'\circ \phi) \end{align*}
    is a bijection, then \(G\cong \prod G_\alpha\text{.}\)

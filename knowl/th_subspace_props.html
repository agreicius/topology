Theorem 2.6.1. Subspace properties.

Let \(Y\) be a subspace of \((X,\mathcal{T})\text{.}\)
  1. If \(\mathcal{B}\) is a basis of \(\mathcal{T}\text{,}\) then the set \(\mathcal{B}_Y=\{B\cap Y\colon B\in\mathcal{B}\}\) is a basis for \(Y\text{.}\)
  2. \(C\subseteq Y\) is closed in \(Y\) if and only if \(C=C'\cap Y\) for some \(C'\subseteq X\) that is closed in \(X\text{.}\)
  3. If \(X\) is \(T_1\) (resp. Hausdorff, resp. metrizable), then \(Y\) is \(T_1\) (resp., Hausdorff, resp. metrizable).

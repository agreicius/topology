Assume \(g,h\) are liftings of \(f\) to \(Y\text{,}\) satisfying \(g(x_0)=h(x_0)\text{.}\) Let \(A=\{x\in X\colon g(x)=h(x)\}\subseteq X\text{.}\) We will show that \(A\) is open and closed. Since \(A\ne \emptyset\) (\(x_0\in A\)) and \(X\) is connected, it follows that \(A=X\) (2.12.4) and hence that \(g=h\text{.}\)

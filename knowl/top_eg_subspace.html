Topological specimen 8. Subspace.

Given a topological space \((X,\mathcal{T})\) and a subset \(Y\subseteq X\) the set \(\mathcal{T}_Y\) defined as
\begin{equation*} \mathcal{T}_Y=\{U\cap Y\colon U\in \mathcal{T}\} \end{equation*}
defines a topology on \(Y\) called the subspace topology. We call \(Y\text{,}\) together with the topology \(\mathcal{T}_Y\text{,}\) a subspace of \(X\text{.}\)

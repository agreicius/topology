Let \(X=U\cup V\) be an open covering where \(U\text{,}\) \(V\text{,}\) and \(U\cap V\) are path connected. In the diagram below, each of the homomorphisms \(i_1,i_2,j_1,j_2\) of fundamental groups is induced by the corresponding inclusion of topological spaces. We have
\begin{equation*} \pi_1(X,x_0)\cong \pi_1(U,x_0)\sqcup_{\pi_1(U\cap V, x_0)} \pi_1(V,x_0)\text{.} \end{equation*}
In more detail, given any group \(H\) and homomorphisms \(\phi_1\colon \pi_1(U,x_0)\rightarrow H\) and \(\phi_2\colon \pi_1(V,x_0)\rightarrow H\) satisfying \(\phi_1\circ i_1=\phi_2\circ i_2\text{,}\) there is a unique homomorphism \(\phi\colon \pi_1(X,x_0)\rightarrow H\) satisfying \(\phi_k=\phi\circ j_k\) for \(1\leq k\leq 2\text{.}\)

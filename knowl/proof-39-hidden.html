Why is \(\rank G\) well-defined? As you will show in a homework exercise, if \(G\cong\oplus_{\alpha\in I}\Z\text{,}\) then \(G/2G\cong \oplus_{k\in I}\Z/2\Z\text{.}\) Thinking of \(\Z/2\Z\) as the finite field \(\F_2\text{,}\) we see that \(\abs{I}\) is just the dimension of \(G/2G\) as a \(\F_2\)-vector space: i.e., \(\rank G=\dim_{\F_2}G/2G\text{.}\)

Theorem 2.17.6. Local compactness equivalence.

Let \(X\) be a Hausdorff space. The following are equivalent.
  1. \(X\) is locally compact.
  2. For all \(x\in X\text{,}\) and for all open sets \(U\) containing \(x\text{,}\) there is an open neighborhood \(V\) of \(x\) such that \(\overline{V}\subseteq U\) and \(\overline{V}\) is compact.

Let \(g_1\colon G_0\rightarrow G_1\) and \(g_2\colon G_0\rightarrow G_2\) be group homomorphisms.
  1. If \(G\) and \(G'\) are pushouts of the homomorphisms \(g_k\colon G_0\rightarrow G_k\text{,}\) then \(G\cong G'\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(G_1*G_2\) be a free product of \(G_1\) and \(G_2\text{,}\) and identify \(G_1\) and \(G_2\) as subgroups of \(G_1*G_2\) as usual. The quotient \(G_1*G_2/N\) is a pushout of the homomorphisms \(g_k\colon G_0\rightarrow G_k\text{,}\) where \(N\) is the least normal subgroup of \(G_1*G_2\) containing the set
    \begin{equation*} \{\left(g_1(g)\right)^{-1}g_2(g)\colon g\in G_0\}\text{.} \end{equation*}

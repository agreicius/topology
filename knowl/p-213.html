When \(X=\emptyset\text{,}\) we have \(\mathcal{P}(X)=\{\emptyset\}\text{.}\) Since by axiom (1) a topology \(\mathcal{T}\) on \(X\) must contain both \(\emptyset\) and \(X=\emptyset\text{,}\) we see the only option is \(\mathcal{T}=\{\emptyset\}=\mathcal{P}(X)\text{.}\) We showed that \(\mathcal{P}(X)\) is always a topology on \(X\) in Topological specimenÂ 2. Thus \(\mathcal{T}=\{\emptyset\}\) is the unique choice of topology for \(X=\emptyset\text{.}\) (Note: in this case the trivial and discrete topologies are equal.)

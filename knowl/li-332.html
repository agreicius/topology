Item 2.

Let \(U_1, U_2\) be disjoint open sets satisfying \(Y=U_1\cup U_2\text{.}\) Take any \(y\in \bigcap_{i\in I}Y_i\text{.}\) We may assume without loss of generality that \(y\in U_1\text{.}\) Now since \(Y_i\) is connected for all \(i\) we must have \(Y_i\subseteq U_1\) or \(Y\subseteq U_2\) by (1). Since \(y\in Y_i\) and \(y\in U_1\text{,}\) it follows that \(Y_i\subseteq U_1\) for all \(i\in I\text{.}\) Thus \(Y=\bigcup_{i\in I}Y_i\subseteq U_1\text{.}\) We conclude that \(U_1=Y\text{.}\) Thus \(Y\) is connected.

Now consider \(T_n\text{.}\) Let \(X=\{a_1,b_1,a_2,b_2,\dots, a_n,b_n\}\text{,}\) let \(F(X)\) be the free group on \(X\text{,}\) let \(Y=\{[a_1,b_1][a_2,b_2]\cdots [a_n,b_n]\}\text{,}\) and let \(q\colon F(X)/\lns(Y)\rightarrow (F(X)/\lns(Y))^{\ab}\) be the quotient map. We have
\begin{align*} (\pi_1(T_n,p))^{\ab} \amp \cong (F(\{a_1,b_1,\dots, a_n,b_n\})/\lns(Y))^{\ab}\\ \amp \cong (F(X))^{\ab}/q(\lns(Y)) \amp \knowl{./knowl/th_abelianization_of_quotient.html}{\text{1.20.3}}\\ \amp \cong (F(X))^{\ab}/\lns(q(Y)) \amp \text{(see claim)}\\ \amp \cong F(X)^{\ab}\text{,} \end{align*}
where the last line follows since \(q([w,w'])=e\in F(X)^{\ab}\) for any elements \(w,w'\in F(X)\text{.}\) Finally, by TheoremÂ 1.14.11 \(F(X)^{\ab}\) is a free abelian group of rank \(2n\text{:}\) and in fact, the image of the system of free generators \(\{a_1,b_1,\dots, a_n,b_n\}\) is a basis of \(F(X)^{\ab}\) as a free abelian group. We conclude that \((\pi_1(T_n,p))^{\ab}\cong \Z^{2n}\text{,}\) as claimed.

Definition 1.12.1.

A separation of a topological space \(X\) is a pair of disjoint nonempty open sets \(U_1, U_2\) satisfying \(X=U_1\cup U_2\text{.}\) The space \(X\) is connected it it admits no separation, and disconnected otherwise.
A subset \(Y\subseteq X\) is connected (resp., disconnected) if it is connected (resp., disconnected) with respect to the subspace topology.

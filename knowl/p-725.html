In both cases (box and product) let \(\mathcal{B}\) be the corresponding basis, as defined in Topological specimen 10. We have
\begin{align*} U \text{ open in } A \amp \iff \text{ for all } x\in U, x\in B\cap A\subseteq U, B=\prod_{i\in I}U_i\in \mathcal{B} \\ \amp\iff \text{ for all } x\in U, x\in \prod_{i\in I} U_i\cap A \subseteq U, B=\prod_{i\in I}U_i \in \mathcal{B} \text{.} \end{align*}
I claim that (in both box and product topology) the last line is equivalent to \(U\) being open in the box or product topology on the product of the subspaces \(A_i\text{.}\) Indeed it says that \(U\) is a union of elements of the form \(\prod_{i\in I}U_i\cap A\text{,}\) where by definition of the standard bases in Topological specimen 10 we have \(U_i\) open in \(X_i\text{,}\) and in the case where we use the product topology, \(U_i=X_i\) for all but finitely many \(i\in I\text{.}\) This is equivalent to \(\prod_{i\in I}U_i\cap A\) being a basis element in either the box topology on the product of the subspaces or the product topology on the product of the subspaces.
